Your Day at Mahua House

A Typical Forest Day

No schedules. No alarms. Just a gentle rhythm set by the forest itself. Here's what a day at Mahua House can look like.

Plan Your Stay
6:30 AM — Sunrise Birdwatching

Greet the day with our naturalist guide as the forest comes alive — peacocks, Indian rollers, painted storks and the chorus of the dawn chorus.

8:00 AM — Farm Breakfast

A leisurely farm-to-table breakfast on the verandah — seasonal fruits, hot parathas, local honey, and filter coffee as the forest hums around you.

10:00 AM — Forest Trail Walk

Take a guided walk through the Samardha forest. Learn about medicinal plants, spot wildlife tracks, and breathe the cleanest air you've had in years.

2:00 PM —Pool Time

Unwind with an Ayurvedic massage, float in the natural-edge pool, or simply read in a hammock as the afternoon light filters through the trees.

7:00 PM — Campfire & Folk Music

As darkness falls, gather around the campfire. Folk musicians play, stories are shared, dinner is served under a canopy of stars. Sleep comes easy here.
